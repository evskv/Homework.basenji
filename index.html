In fact, they do. Like most hounds, Basenjis are very vocal. How(l)ever, unlike with most dogs, one can hardly call it a bark. The unique noise they make can be best described as a yodel. Just listen to these majestic melodies

Basenjis often stand on their hind legs, somewhat like a meerkat, by themselves or leaning on something; this behavior is often observed when the dog is curious about something.

Clever and endearing, these dogs are good companions for the person or family who can stay a step ahead of them. They can adapt to apartment living, and even novice pet parents will find these pups to be great first-timer dogs. Just make sure you can provide enough exercise to meet their high energy levels.
